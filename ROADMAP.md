# Roadmap

Open Typing Trainer is an international open-source foundation for touch typing education. The current Russian/Cyrillic seed pack proves the first training flow; the long-term goal is a trainer that communities can localize for their own language, keyboard, school, and workflow needs.

## Near Term

- Stabilize the core lesson flow, virtual keyboard, typing metrics, and desktop-first layout.
- Keep the Russian/Cyrillic seed pack polished and consistent, including `зн/мин` speed display.
- Document the expected shape of language packs, keyboard layouts, fonts, and lesson data.
- Improve accessibility, responsive fallback behavior, and keyboard-only navigation.
- Prepare contribution paths for schools, local communities, and language maintainers.

## Language and Layout Expansion

- Add a language-pack system that can support multiple alphabets, keyboard layouts, fonts, and lesson sequences.
- Support community-maintained packs for different countries and education systems.
- Make locale-specific metrics, punctuation, typography, and keyboard conventions configurable.
- Provide validation tools for lesson text, keyboard coverage, and progression quality.

## AI-Era Typing Lessons

- Add lessons for prompt writing, slash commands, terminal commands, Markdown, JSON, Git, and code snippets.
- Include practical exercises for modern workflows such as chat-based tools, developer consoles, and structured text editing.
- Support mixed natural-language and technical typing drills without making the product developer-only.

## Gaze Discipline Training

- Build camera-assisted detection for moments when a learner looks down at the keyboard instead of keeping attention on the screen.
- Surface gaze discipline as part of lesson feedback so learners can understand when they are relying on visual key search.
- Keep this feature privacy-first: explicit consent, visible camera state, local processing where feasible, no video recording by default, and clear classroom/child-safety boundaries.
- Treat gaze feedback as coaching, not punishment or surveillance.

## Product Direction

- Evolve toward a polished macOS-like desktop app experience while keeping the web foundation open and portable.
- Keep the project friendly to educators, students, open-source communities, and independent language maintainers.
- Maintain a simple architecture that makes localization and lesson contribution approachable.
